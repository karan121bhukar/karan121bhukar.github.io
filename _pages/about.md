I am an **Applied Scientist at [Amazon](https://www.amazon.science/)**, working on multilingual search query understanding. I train encoder–decoder models that unify spell correction and entity slotting across English, Spanish, and Portuguese, shipping a single model for the US, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil marketplaces.

Previously I was a Research Engineer at [IBM Research](https://research.ibm.com/labs/india), where I built **BERTOps** — a domain-specialized masked-LM for semi-structured IT logs — and the **Dynamic-X-Y** alert-suppression system that shipped across IBM's AIOps platforms, cutting non-actionable alerts by around 90% in internal deployments. That work produced publications at ICSE, AAAI, IEEE CLOUD, ICPE, and COMSNETS, plus a growing patent portfolio in the U.S., China, and Japan.

I did my B.Tech in Computer Science at [IIT Hyderabad](https://www.iith.ac.in/) (2018–2022, CGPA 9.04/10.00). During that time I spent summers at IBM Research (as a Blue Scholar intern) and ServiceNow, and contributed to the autonomous-driving benchmark work in [Lab 1055](https://lab1055.github.io/) under Profs. Vineeth N. Balasubramanian and C. V. Jawahar.

My research interests sit at the intersection of **reinforcement learning, information extraction, multilingual NLP, and log analytics** — I'm drawn to problems where improving the model is only half the fight and the other half is turning the research into something that actually runs in production.

You can reach me at **[choudhary121karan@gmail.com](mailto:choudhary121karan@gmail.com)**, read the full [CV](/cv/), or browse [publications and patents](/publications/).
